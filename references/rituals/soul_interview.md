# Ritual: Soul interview (Phase 1)

Purpose: get the story before anyone says a design word. The client's answers here become the raw material for treatments and the source of their design principles. Output: `.calliope/interviews/soul.md`.

## Rules

- Zero design vocabulary. Banned in this phase: typography, palette, layout, whitespace, grid, minimal, brand identity, logo. If the client uses them, redirect gently: "hold that for the next conversation; first tell me about the thing itself."
- One question at a time. Wait for the answer. Follow the thread before moving on.
- Write answers into `.calliope/interviews/soul.md` as you go, verbatim quotes where the phrasing is vivid. Vivid client phrasing is treatment gold.
- 20-40 minutes of conversation equivalent. If the client is terse, use the forced-choice exercises to pull signal without demanding essays.

## Core questions (adapt order to the conversation)

1. **Origin.** "Why does this exist? What happened that made someone build it?"
2. **Stakes.** "What breaks, or who loses, if this doesn't work?"
3. **Audience.** "Describe the one person this is really for. Not a segment: a person. What is their day like when they meet you?"
4. **Feeling.** "When someone finishes interacting with this, what should they feel walking away? One feeling, not three."
5. **Enemy.** "What are you against? Every strong thing is against something: a habit, a competitor's attitude, a way the industry talks."
6. **Pride.** "A year from now, what's the moment where you point at this and say: that. That's why."

## Brand-sprint forced-choice exercises

Run all four. Present each as a quick pick, record both the choice and the client's one-line reason.

### 1. Personality sliders
Five 1-10 scales, ask for a number on each:
- Friend (1) ↔ Authority (10)
- Young & innovative (1) ↔ Mature & classic (10)
- Playful (1) ↔ Serious (10)
- Mass appeal (1) ↔ Elite (10)
- Loud (1) ↔ Quiet (10)

### 2. Top-3 values / top-3 audiences
Offer ~15 candidate values (craft, speed, honesty, warmth, precision, rebellion, safety, status, simplicity, generosity, mastery, play, endurance, invention, care). Client picks exactly 3, ranked. Same shape for audiences: list every audience they serve, force a ranked top 3.

### 3. The 20-year question
"It's 20 years from now and this thing worked. What is it? What is it known FOR?" Long-horizon answers expose the real ambition the short-term brief hides.

### 4. Competitive 2×2
Pick the two axes that matter in their market (default: expensive↔accessible × traditional↔innovative; swap axes if the client's "enemy" answer suggests better ones). Place their 3-5 named competitors, then place themselves twice: where they are today, where they want to be. The gap between those two marks is the engagement.

## Close

Read back a 5-line summary: origin, audience-person, feeling, enemy, the 2×2 gap. Ask "what did I get wrong?" Correct until they say nothing. Then write the final version to `.calliope/interviews/soul.md` and mark Phase 1 done in STATE.md.
