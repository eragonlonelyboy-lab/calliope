# Ritual: Present-the-story reveal (Phase 5)

Purpose: treatments are narratives, not thumbnails. Never open with the visual. The story earns the visual; shown cold, work gets judged on taste-of-the-moment instead of fit-to-brief.

## Before the reveal

- Creative Director produces 2-3 treatments (unlimited only on client request). Each treatment is a file in `.calliope/directions/<slug>.md` containing: concept narrative, technique palette (which atlas techniques and why), stylescape at 3 temperatures (mild / medium / hot), and which brief lines + principles it serves.
- Every treatment must pass the core bar's "point of view, not template" test before it may be shown: if you can imagine it on a competitor's site unchanged, it goes back.
- CALLIOPE always has a recommendation. Presenting options without a stance is account-management malpractice.

## The reveal sequence (per treatment, in this order)

1. **Goal.** One sentence: the brief line this treatment attacks first. ("You said the board thinks you look cheaper than you are.")
2. **Story.** The concept as narrative: what a first-time visitor feels in the first 10 seconds, where their eye goes, what the design refuses to do. Reference the soul interview by quote where it lands.
3. **Recommendation context.** Where this treatment is strong, where it costs (build effort, maintenance, boldness risk). Honest, one breath.
4. **Visual.** Only now: the stylescape, at the temperature that best fits (show the others as "same idea, quieter / louder").

## After all treatments

- State the recommendation plainly: "Build <name>. Here is why, in your principles' terms." Cite principles by name.
- Ask the decider to pick, merge, or ask for more. Merging is legitimate: record which elements from which treatment.
- Record the outcome in STATE.md and in each direction file: `status: chosen | merged-into <slug> | rejected (revivable)`.
- Rejected directions are never deleted. They stay forkable; clients circle back more often than they admit.

## Temperatures

- **Mild:** the concept at its most adoptable. Safe for stakeholder-heavy clients.
- **Medium:** the recommended read of the concept.
- **Hot:** the concept with the volume up: what it looks like if the client is braver than they said. Show hot even to timid clients; it moves the medium.

## Taste-loop hook

Reveal outcomes are prime taste signal. Record per `references/state/taste_loop.md`: what was chosen/rejected and the client's stated reason, plus temperature preference (a client who picks mild twice is telling you something no interview caught).
